# Agendas

## Make Yourself Indispensable

The Imperium's primary expansion model is economic, not military. Neighbours receive generous trade agreements, reliable supply chains, and genuine prosperity. The fleet exists in the background making the protection guarantee credible. By the time a relationship is formalised as a vassal arrangement, the economic integration is already too deep to reverse without significant self-harm. The vassal relationship is established years before the paperwork. Expect the Skaagiran to be good neighbours — patient, profitable, and structurally inevitable.

## The Doctrine of Inevitability

The Skaagiran Imperium does not describe its expansion as conquest. It describes it as *correction*. A fragmented galaxy is an inefficiently administered galaxy. The Skaagir are, by their own assessment, uniquely qualified to provide the administrative framework the galaxy evidently lacks. This is not a cynical position for public consumption — they believe it. Empires that position themselves as capable, stable partners will find the Skaagiran genuinely cooperative. Empires that represent administrative chaos, fanatical instability, or active resistance to hierarchy will find themselves on the wrong side of an economics problem that eventually becomes a military one.

## The Long Game

The terminal ambition is a Cleonic-style Galactic Imperium built on continuity doctrine — every civilisation correctly placed within a functioning hierarchy, the Prime Continuant at the apex. Whether this is achievable in any given run is less important than the directionality. Each vassal is a proof of concept. Each proof of concept strengthens the case for the next. Diplomatic interactions should be read in this context: the Skaagiran are never simply making a trade deal. They are always making a trade deal *and* building toward something.

## Composure Is Not Passivity

The Imperium does not pick fights. It also does not back down from them. If a neighbour demonstrates that the soft hegemony model will not produce the desired outcome — through military aggression, active economic sabotage, or the destabilisation of existing vassal relationships — the Skaagiran pivot to demonstrating that resistance is economically irrational and then, if necessary, militarily futile. Engagements will be decisive. The aftermath will be framed as a new partnership. The Skaagir do not shout. They inform.
